## ⚠️ Advisory (read first)

1. **Effective date Jan 1, 2026 is NOT available.** Under IRC §1362(d)(1)(C)–(D), a revocation is
   effective the first day of the tax year only if filed **on or before the 15th day of the 3rd month**
   of that year (March 15, 2026 for a calendar-year corp). Today is **June 29, 2026** — that window has
   closed. A revocation filed now is effective **January 1, 2027** (default), unless you specify a
   prospective date in 2026, which splits 2026 into an S short-year + a C short-year (messy and
   generally undesirable). **This statement is prepared for an effective date of January 1, 2027.**

2. **Revoking the S-election alone makes the LLC a C-corporation, not a disregarded entity.** When
   Sweet Home filed Form 2553 (S-election eff. 1/1/2024), it made a *deemed* election to be classified
   as an association taxable as a corporation. Revoking only the S-election leaves that corporate
   classification in place → it would become a **C-corp**. To return it to a **disregarded single-member
   LLC** (rentals on the owner's Schedule E, the goal), you must **also file Form 8832** electing
   disregarded/default classification effective the same date.

3. **60-month limitation caveat (Form 8832).** Treas. Reg. §301.7701-3(c)(1)(iv) generally bars a new
   entity-classification election within **60 months** of a prior one. The deemed election was effective
   1/1/2024, so the 60-month period runs to ~1/1/2029. A 1/1/2027 Form 8832 may be **within** that window
   — confirm whether an exception applies (e.g., >50% ownership change) or whether IRS consent is needed.
   *Net: a CPA/EA should confirm the 8832 mechanics before filing.* The S-election **revocation** below
   is not subject to the 60-month rule and can be filed independently.
